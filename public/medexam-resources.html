Top Tips

10 Essential UCAT Tips

1
Start early — 6–8 weeks minimum
The UCAT tests cognitive abilities that improve with practice. Most students who score in the top quartile began practising at least 6 weeks before their test date.
2
Always attempt every question
There is no negative marking in the UCAT. A blank answer is always wrong — a guess gives you a chance. Never leave a question blank, especially in AR.
3
Practice under timed conditions
Many students improve dramatically once they switch from untimed to timed practice. Use our platform's timed mode from week 3 onwards to build speed.
4
Understand what each subtest tests
VR tests reading comprehension, not general knowledge. QR tests data interpretation, not advanced maths. AR tests pattern recognition, not art. Tailor your approach to each.
5
Review your wrong answers carefully
Don't just do questions — understand why you got them wrong. Our platform provides detailed explanations for every answer. This is where most improvement happens.
6
Flag and move on
The UCAT has a flagging feature. If a question is taking too long, flag it, move on, and come back at the end. Never spend more than 90 seconds on any single question.
7
Do at least 2 full mock exams
The stamina required to maintain focus for 2+ hours is underestimated. Practise the full exam under realistic conditions at least twice before test day.
8
Use the on-screen calculator for QR
You must use the UCAT's on-screen calculator — no physical calculators are allowed. Practise using it for speed. Learn keyboard shortcuts if possible.
9
Maintain wellbeing during prep
Sleep, exercise, and mental breaks are not wasted time — they are essential for cognitive performance. Burnout before the exam will hurt your score.
10
Know the test centre rules
Arrive 30 minutes early. Bring valid photo ID. No notes, phones, or revision materials. You will be given a laminated notepad and pen for working. Know what to expect.
Subtest Strategies

Strategies by Subtest

VR
Verbal Reasoning
44 questions · 22 min · ~30 sec per question
Read the question firstIt tells you what to look for in the passage. Don't read the whole passage first.
True / False / Can't TellCan't Tell means the passage neither confirms nor denies. Don't use outside knowledge.
Use keyword scanningFind the relevant sentence in the passage using keywords from the question. Read just that part.
Don't overthinkVR answers are always directly supported by the text. If it's not in the passage, it's Can't Tell.
DM
Decision Making
29 questions · 31 min · ~64 sec per question
SyllogismsDraw Venn diagrams for all/some/no questions. This prevents logical errors.
Probability questionsSet up the calculation before answering. Don't rely on intuition for numbers.
Argument evaluationIdentify the conclusion and premises. Does the new information weaken or strengthen the logic?
Statistical reasoningWatch for averages vs totals, percentages vs raw numbers — common trick question types.
QR
Quantitative Reasoning
36 questions · 25 min · ~42 sec per question
Read the table/graph firstUnderstand what units are used and what each column/row represents before answering.
Estimate where possibleMany QR questions can be answered with an estimate. Don't calculate precisely unless the answer choices are close.
Check your unitsConversion errors (kg to g, miles to km) are a common source of mistakes in QR.
Percentage questionsKnow: percentage change = (new - old)/old × 100. This formula appears very frequently.
AR
Abstract Reasoning
50 questions · 12 min · ~14 sec per question
SCANS methodCheck Shape, Colour, Arrangement, Number, Size systematically for each set.
Never spend more than 20 secondsIf you don't see the pattern quickly, guess and move on. Come back if time allows.
Look for the simplest rule firstAR rules are usually simple (e.g. number of shapes = always 3). Don't overthink.
Practise dailyAR improves faster than any other subtest with regular short practice sessions. 15 min/day is ideal.
SJT
Situational Judgement
69 questions · 26 min · ~23 sec per question
Patient safety firstIn any scenario involving patient harm, this always takes priority over everything else.
Know GMC Good Medical PracticeMost SJT scenarios test the values in this document. Read it before your exam.
Appropriateness vs importanceKnow the difference between rating how appropriate an action is vs how important it is.
Don't pick extremes by defaultMany appropriate responses are "appropriate but not ideal" — not "very appropriate" or "very inappropriate".
Study Schedule

6-Week UCAT Study Plan

Assuming 1–2 hours of study per day. Adjust based on your timeline.

Week 1
Verbal Reasoning
Learn True/False/Can't Tell logic. Practice 20 untimed passages. Study keyword scanning technique. Do 1 timed VR session by end of week.
Week 2
Decision Making
Study syllogism and Venn diagram methods. Practice probability and statistical reasoning. Work through 30 DM questions with full review of explanations.
Week 3
Quantitative Reasoning
Practise on-screen calculator. Learn to read tables and graphs quickly. Focus on percentage change, ratios, and unit conversions. Do 1 timed QR session.
Week 4
Abstract & SJT
Learn SCANS method for AR. Do 15 min daily AR practice. Read GMC Good Medical Practice. Work through 30 SJT questions with detailed review.
Week 5
Mixed Practice & Weak Areas
Do mixed questions across all subtests daily. Identify your 2 weakest subtests from analytics. Dedicate extra time to those. Complete Mock Exam 1 this week.
Week 6
Full Mock & Final Polish
Complete Mock Exam 2 under full exam conditions. Review errors. Maintain stamina with daily practice. Reduce volume 2 days before exam. Rest the day before.
